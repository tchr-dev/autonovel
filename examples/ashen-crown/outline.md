# Outline — Ashen Crown

## Structure

- **Total:** 12 chapters, target ~30,000 words.
- **Per-chapter target:** ~2,500 words (range 2,200–2,900 — short chapters earn the white space).
- **POV:** Rowan, third-limited past, throughout.
- **Acts:**
  - **Act 1 (Ch 1–4):** Discovery. Rowan finds the crown, is brought to court, learns there is an egg.
  - **Act 2 (Ch 5–9):** Pilgrimage. The road to Cradle Shrine, the oath, the hatching, the first costs.
  - **Act 3 (Ch 10–12):** Refusal and negotiation. Caldus's death, the parley, the oath-breaking.

## Themes

- **Ownership vs. partnership.** Every faction wants to *own* the dragon. The book's argument is that ownership is the disease, not the cure.
- **What love costs.** The memory ladder makes love measurable. The book asks whether you can love someone you can no longer remember loving.
- **What survives a country that loses its myth.** Vaelgard has been emptier for three hundred winters and didn't quite know it. The dragon's return reveals the absence; freeing it again means living with that absence honestly.
- **The chosen-one trap.** Rowan was chosen — but by Caldus, manipulatively, not by destiny. The book makes the reader feel that distinction.

## Act 1: The Crown in the Grave (Ch 1–4)

### Ch 1: "Frozen Ground" (Rowan POV, ~2,500 words) — STATUS: drafted (v2, score 7.7)
- **BEATS:**
  1. Open at Brann's Forge: Rowan finishing a quench, Brann's praise. Establish his hands, his master's voice, the smell of slag and pine smoke. Establish that he can't quite remember the smell of his mother's hair (he thinks: just time).
  2. A king's reeve passes through the village asking after old battlefield iron — the king is buying scrap. Brann sends Rowan to Marrow Field with a barrow and instructions to bring back what he can.
  3. Rowan walks the day to Marrow Field. Snow. The sensory hook: cold iron, pine smoke, snow on red banners (a refugee's small mourning-banner tied to a wayside cairn).
  4. He digs at a shallow grave that is too shallow for an honest burial. Bone first, then iron — a black ring, broken, three points missing, no jewels. (Default-mode prose throughout.)
  5. His glove tears. The iron is warm. Old-tongue mode flickers on for one paragraph: *three hundred winters,* the thought arriving already finished. Then it leaves him. He is shaking.
  6. End on him not putting the crown in the barrow with the rest of the scrap. He puts it in his coat. He does not know why.
- **PLANTS:**
  - Black glass and a bell in his sleep that night. (Payoff: Ch 6 / Ch 9.)
  - Brann's "you'll know what you're for when something asks for you, not when something gives you a job." (Payoff: Ch 11.)
  - Can't recall mother's hair. (Payoff: Ch 7.)
- **HARVESTS:** None — opening chapter.
- **EMOTIONAL ARC:** Routine pride → unease → silent fear.
- **STATUS:** unwritten

### Ch 2: "What the Reeve Said" (Rowan POV, ~2,300 words)
- **BEATS:**
  1. Rowan returns to Brann's Forge with the barrow. He does not show Brann the crown.
  2. Dream: black glass, a bell, his own voice in a register that isn't his.
  3. The reeve returns, looking specifically for *broken iron, ring-shaped*, and offering twice the weight in silver. The reeve's questions are too specific. Rowan lies. Brann sees him lie and says nothing.
  4. Brann, that night: tells Rowan he doesn't want to know what he is hiding, but if it is iron the king is asking for, *the king will come*. Rowan understands he has to leave the village before the king arrives; staying puts Brann at risk.
  5. Rowan packs at dawn. Brann gives him a sword — the first he has been allowed to carry on the road, not just hammer-make. Brann's farewell is short. Rowan loses, briefly, the ability to hear the rhythm of his master's speech as he walks down the road. He thinks: just the cold. (It is not.)
- **PLANTS:**
  - The reeve's specific questions imply someone in court is looking for *this exact crown*. (Payoff: Ch 4, Aldren.)
  - The dream pattern. (Payoff: Ch 7, the hatching scene re-uses the same imagery.)
- **HARVESTS:** Mother's hair (Ch 1) re-mentioned to anchor it before Ch 7 takes it.
- **EMOTIONAL ARC:** Domestic safety → severance.
- **STATUS:** unwritten

### Ch 3: "Hold-on-Iron" (Rowan POV, ~2,700 words)
- **BEATS:**
  1. Four days on the road, compressed. Rowan reaches the capital at dusk.
  2. At the gate: the beggar — an old man with a missing ear, wrapped in a burned-pink cloak that does not quite hide the scars on his neck. Rowan gives him a coin he can't quite afford. The beggar's eyes follow him longer than a beggar's eyes should. (The reader does not know yet: this is Caldus's man. Caldus has paid him a winter-coin a winter for ten winters to watch the gate for a smith's apprentice with the right hands. He recognises Rowan and that night sends word north by carter.) Mira is in the line behind Rowan at the gate, her hood up. They do not speak.
  3. Inside the city: Rowan tries to sell scrap iron at the smith-quarter to fund a room. The buyer asks if he is *that* boy from Brann's, the one the reeve was after. Rowan understands he has been described.
  4. Mira intercepts him in an alley. She has been watching him since the gate. She knows what he is carrying because she has been watching for a week for someone exactly like him. She does not ask to see it; she asks him to walk with her, somewhere she has hidden a fire. He goes because he has run out of better choices.
  5. At her hideout: she tells him she is hunting dragon-relics. She does not tell him she means to destroy them. She asks him what he found. He shows her, hands shaking. She holds it for one long heartbeat. Old-tongue mode does not fire — she is not bonded — but she reacts as if she has touched something. She gives it back.
  6. They are interrupted: the king's guard at the door. Mira blames Rowan with her eyes; the guard is here because Rowan was followed from the smith-quarter, not because of her. They are taken to the king.
- **PLANTS:**
  - The beggar at the gate. (Payoff: Ch 10.)
  - Mira's silver coin in her boot. (Payoff: Ch 11.)
- **HARVESTS:**
  - Reeve's specific questions (Ch 2) → confirmed: the king is the one looking.
- **EMOTIONAL ARC:** Wary travel → false safety → arrest.
- **STATUS:** unwritten

### Ch 4: "The King's Hands" (Rowan POV, ~2,800 words)
- **BEATS:**
  1. Aldren's hall. Red collars, grey wool, Aldren on the throne with a tremor in his left hand he is doing his best to hide. Mira will not look at him.
  2. The crown is laid on a cloth on a table between Rowan and the king. Aldren does not pick it up. Rowan understands, slowly, that Aldren *cannot* — the king is afraid. (Defensible reading: the corrupted oath he has been preparing requires a different bond-bearer, not himself.)
  3. Aldren tells Rowan, with the privacy of his three council lords (Reska, Olen, Brae) and Mira, what the crown is. He gives the corrupted history: a tool of the kings, lost, recovered, owed. He tells Rowan there is an egg. He does not say where. He says the kingdom needs both, and Rowan is the bearer because *the iron chose his hand and not another's*.
  4. Aldren names the corrupted oath. Caldus's name surfaces — *the keeper of the shrine* — but Aldren refers to him as a forty-winter recluse, suggests Rowan will be guided by him. Aldren's tone is wrong. Rowan does not know enough to know why.
  5. Mira speaks for the first time, briefly: she will go with Rowan because she has seen too many smiths' apprentices die alone on the king's roads. Aldren accepts this with a courtesy that fools no one. (Aldren intends Mira to kill the egg if it cannot be bound. He will tell her this in private.)
  6. Aldren says, almost to himself, *the shrines were not built for kings.* Rowan hears it. He does not know it is the chapter's most important line.
  7. Final beat: a private moment — Aldren and Rowan alone for thirty seconds. Aldren puts his hand on Rowan's shoulder. He looks at Rowan as if he is seeing his own self at seventeen. He says, *come back to me with the dragon.* Rowan kneels — first kneeling.
- **PLANTS:**
  - Aldren's tremor. (Payoff: Ch 12.)
  - "The shrines were not built for kings." (Payoff: Ch 9.)
  - Aldren's private orders to Mira. (Payoff: Ch 9, when Mira disobeys.)
- **HARVESTS:**
  - The reeve thread closes (Ch 2/3): the king was indeed the buyer.
- **EMOTIONAL ARC:** Awe → unease → first kneeling.
- **STATUS:** unwritten

## Act 2: The Pilgrimage (Ch 5–9)

### Ch 5: "Caldus" (Rowan POV, ~2,500 words)
- **BEATS:**
  1. Three days on the road, Rowan and Mira. Quiet. They do not yet trust each other; the silences are the chapter's primary texture.
  2. They climb. Pine thins, scree begins. They reach the Pass of Caer Vael; the wind sounds wrong. Rowan dreams again — bell, black glass, *plus a new image*: a man with a burned face standing at a stone door.
  3. They descend a half-day to Cradle Shrine. The shrine is a single black-basalt room. Caldus is outside it, splitting wood with a stick to lever the axe.
  4. First exchange: Caldus does not turn. He says, "the road told me." Rowan asks what the road told him. Caldus says, "that you would come and that you would be young." *(Reader-visible tell: Caldus answers without looking up from the wood, and when Mira asks the same question half a page later, he gives the same words in the same order — too rehearsed. The first reading parses it as a stoic old man's habit. The Ch 10 reveal recasts it: he had been practising this answer for forty winters.)*
  5. Inside the shrine: the oath-stone. Rowan does not yet know to look at it. Caldus makes them eat. He asks Mira who taught her bowyering. She names the southern bowyer. Caldus's eyes change. He had served with that man.
  6. That night, Caldus holds the crown briefly — left hand shaking. Rowan reads it as palsy. (Reader will read it as recognition by Ch 9.) Caldus tells Rowan, plainly, that the dragon-age is over and the egg should have been left to die. Rowan asks where the egg is. Caldus says, "behind that wall," and points. Rowan sleeps badly.
- **PLANTS:**
  - Caldus's "the road told me." (Payoff: Ch 10, the journal.)
  - Caldus's left-hand tremor on the crown. (Payoff: Ch 9 understanding.)
  - Mira's "I shoot what I'm pointed at." (Payoff: Ch 9 inversion.)
- **HARVESTS:**
  - The dream pattern (Ch 1/2) deepens with the burned man → recognised in Caldus.
- **EMOTIONAL ARC:** Climbing dread → arrival → false rest.
- **STATUS:** unwritten

### Ch 6: "What the Stone Says" (Rowan POV, ~2,400 words)
- **BEATS:**
  1. Morning. Caldus opens the back wall of the shrine: a niche, sealed with mortar. Inside: a basalt cradle, and in the cradle, the egg, scales like warm black glass.
  2. Rowan touches it. Old-tongue mode fires — he hears the bell from the dream, this time inside his chest. He hears the egg's heart through the basalt road under the floor. He knows, as if he had always known, that the egg has been dreaming at him for a winter and a half. (The reader gets this before he fully articulates it.)
  3. He pulls his hand back. He is shaken. Caldus watches and does not help.
  4. Mira: silent. She has been arguing with herself since Hold-on-Iron about whether she came here to destroy this. She does not destroy it. She does not know yet why.
  5. Caldus tells Rowan the corrupted oath Aldren taught him is not the oath. The oath has a clause the king's version omits: *consent of the bonded.* He says it almost as throwaway. Rowan does not yet understand what it costs to swear it.
  6. Caldus offers to teach him the oath. He says: "I will not swear it for you. You will swear it. Or you will leave the egg here and walk away, and I will mortar the wall again, and we will none of us speak of this to the king." This is the chapter's choice: a real, refusable choice. Rowan does not refuse. (The reader is meant to wish for a moment that he had.)
  7. End: Rowan, alone, reads the oath text in Caldus's hand. The next chapter is the swearing.
- **PLANTS:**
  - The bell-in-the-chest. (Payoff: Ch 12, the oath-breaking.)
- **HARVESTS:**
  - Dream of black glass and bell (Ch 1) → arrives.
  - Aldren's corrupted oath (Ch 4) → confirmed corrupted.
- **EMOTIONAL ARC:** Reverence → choice → resignation toward swearing.
- **STATUS:** unwritten

### Ch 7: "The Hatching" (Rowan POV, ~2,800 words)
- **BEATS:**
  1. The Ashen Oath at the stone. Caldus speaks the form. Rowan responds. Mira witnesses, hands at her bow but not on it. The crown is on the stone between Rowan and the egg.
  2. The oath in full: language modeled on something between a marriage and a debt-ledger. The consent clause is unmistakable. Rowan agrees to it knowing only that the cost is *memory* and that he will pay; he does not yet know how the bond chooses what to take.
  3. The egg cracks. The dragon comes out, blood-wet, the size of a large dog, scales like warm black glass, eyes the colour of slag. Its shadow is wrong. Mira gasps, once, audibly.
  4. The dragon's first ask: *salt.* Caldus, who had prepared salt at the side of the stone, gives it. (Plant; payoff Ch 12.) The dragon eats. Then it asks for meat, and they give it the rabbit Caldus had killed that morning.
  5. First crown-use: an hour later, on the road back from the shrine to the campsite below the pass. They have to cross a stream. The egg-cradle's basalt was heavy and they are slow; the stream is narrowing them between two slopes where they could be ambushed. Rowan, on impulse, on Caldus's instruction, speaks *cold*. The crown focuses it. The stream slows, then ices over. They cross.
  6. He reaches, casually, half an hour later, for a memory of his master praising his quench — and finds nothing. Not the absence of the memory; the absence of the *loving*. He still knows Brann praised him. He cannot remember the morning or the particular fact. He sits down on a rock. He says nothing. Mira asks. He does not answer. Caldus says: "It begins."
- **PLANTS:**
  - The salt. (Payoff: Ch 12.)
  - The shadow that does not match the body. (Payoff: Ch 11, the parley scene where Varkesh sees it and understands what Rowan has not been told.)
- **HARVESTS:**
  - Mother's hair (Ch 1/2) → reader realises the bond began before the crown.
- **EMOTIONAL ARC:** Sacred terror → wonder → first grief.
- **STATUS:** unwritten

### Ch 8: "The Burning Farmhouse" (Rowan POV, ~2,600 words)
- **BEATS:**
  1. Travel scene: descending the southern slope. The dragon clings to Rowan's shoulder like a falcon at first, then walks, then is left to follow. Mira has been talking to it (carefully); it does not respond in words yet to her, only to Rowan and Caldus.
  2. They detour through a farm village to buy food. Smoke on the horizon — Iron North scouts have been raiding. They reach a farmhouse on fire. Three farm-folk inside, the door blocked by a fallen beam.
  3. Rowan uses the crown a second time: *wind*. Clears the smoke long enough for Mira to break the door with her shoulder. They get the farm-folk out. One of them — a girl Rowan's age — looks at the dragon and does not scream; she has been raised to expect the world to contain things she does not know.
  4. The cost: Rowan loses his sister's laugh. He realises, after, that his sister died when he was eight, and he has carried her laugh in his head ever since, and he does not have it anymore. The reader has never seen her. He tells Mira about her, briefly, by way of mourning the fact that he can no longer hear her. The act of telling her is the closest thing to a love-scene the book has so far.
  5. White-flag scout: while they finish, a single Iron North scout under a white flag rides up to ask for the body of one of his comrades who fell at the village. He gets it. He is northern, exact, slightly formal. (Plant for Ch 11.)
  6. End: Caldus is unwell. He does not say so. Rowan notices. He says nothing.
- **PLANTS:**
  - White-flag courtesy from the Iron North. (Payoff: Ch 11.)
  - Caldus's silent illness. (Payoff: Ch 10, his death.)
- **HARVESTS:**
  - First crown-use's grief (Ch 7) compounded with second.
- **EMOTIONAL ARC:** Action → rescue → second grief, deeper.
- **STATUS:** unwritten

### Ch 9: "What the Stone Will Not Recognise" (Rowan POV, ~2,900 words)
- **BEATS:**
  1. They are recalled to Hold-on-Iron — message from Aldren. The Iron North have begun moving. Aldren wants the dragon at the pass.
  2. They arrive. The hall is crowded. Aldren is worse — his left hand will not be still. He demands the binding ceremony be done in his hall, on his terms, with the corrupted oath. Caldus, terrified, says nothing. The dragon refuses, with a single sentence in old-tongue mode: *we were not made to be sworn to a chair.*
  3. Aldren orders Mira to her duty: shoot the dragon before the night is out, if it cannot be bound. Mira draws. She nocks. She does not shoot. She says, exactly: *I don't shoot what I'm pointed at. Not anymore.* (Plant from Ch 5 inverts.)
  4. The chapter pivots: Rowan, kneeling for the second time before Aldren, refuses on the dragon's behalf. He tells the king: the shrine itself does not recognise *king* as a category. The oath-stone does not know what a throne is. (Plant from Ch 4 harvests.) Aldren does not believe him. Aldren orders him arrested.
  5. Caldus, who has been silent, intervenes: he reveals that the Iron North have crossed the pass already — Varkesh was earlier than anyone thought — and the king's guard are needed at the pass *now*. The arrest is interrupted by necessity.
  6. Rowan goes to the pass with twelve of the king's guard, Mira, Caldus, and the dragon. He calls *stone* to shore the cliff against the flank-attack. The pass holds. Twelve men are alive who would not have been.
  7. The cost, here: the smell of his mother's hair. He goes to recall it the night after, sitting up, alone, with the dragon's breath on his ankle, and the absence is total. He weeps. The reader weeps with him. The reader has been waiting since Ch 1.
- **PLANTS:**
  - Caldus's tremor recognition (deepening of Ch 5 plant).
- **HARVESTS:**
  - "The shrines were not built for kings" (Ch 4) → arrives.
  - "I shoot what I'm pointed at" (Ch 5) → inverts.
  - Mother's hair (Ch 1/2) → taken.
  - Caldus's tremor (Ch 5) → recognised as recognition, not palsy.
- **EMOTIONAL ARC:** Refusal of the king → battle action → the worst grief so far.
- **STATUS:** unwritten

## Act 3: The Oath-Breaking (Ch 10–12)

### Ch 10: "What the Road Told Him" (Rowan POV, ~2,500 words)
- **BEATS:**
  1. Returning from the pass to Cradle Shrine to regroup; Aldren has been left in the keep, fading. Rowan is in disgrace with the court but still alive because the dragon has made him untouchable.
  2. On the road, between the shrine and the ruins, an Iron North scout-party. Caldus, who has been failing all day, sends Rowan ahead with the dragon and the basalt cradle. Mira stays. Caldus dies fighting beside her. Mira buries him in the spot he had told her about, two nights before, like a man finishing a list.
  3. Rowan, ahead, feels Caldus die — not through bond, but through the dragon: the dragon goes still, and then, in old-tongue mode, says, *the one who waited.* Rowan turns back. Mira meets him on the road with the news. He does not weep, this time. He has learned not to spend grief he cannot afford.
  4. At the shrine: Rowan reads Caldus's journal. He works out the dates. He realises Caldus put the crown in the grave, knowing Rowan would walk that road. He was *chosen*. He was *set up*. The book goes silent in his head for a paragraph; then the dragon, who has been listening, says: *and would you have come, if you had not been asked?*
  5. He does not answer. He calls *cold* one more time — needed for tactical reason that night (river-freeze, Mira and the dragon need to cross under cover; he holds the bridge alone). The cost: a specific evening with Mira from Ch 9, a conversation, gone. He realises afterward, when she refers to it.
  6. End: Rowan and Mira and the dragon at Cradle Shrine, no Caldus. They have to decide what to do next. Rowan, for the first time, decides without asking.
- **PLANTS:** None new — convergence chapter.
- **HARVESTS:**
  - The beggar at the gate (Ch 3) → Caldus's journal records the watcher's report by date and burned-pink cloak. Caldus had eyes on Rowan from the moment he passed through the gate. The journal-margin sketch matches the man Rowan gave a coin to.
  - Mira's silver coin (Ch 3) / father's caches → she admits to Rowan she has been finding them.
  - Caldus's "the road told me" (Ch 5) → arrives.
  - Caldus's silent illness (Ch 8) → his death.
- **EMOTIONAL ARC:** Shock → mourning → first sovereign decision.
- **STATUS:** unwritten

### Ch 11: "Parley" (Rowan POV, ~2,700 words)
- **BEATS:**
  1. Mira sends a letter to Varkesh, in her father's name, asking for parley. She writes it without consulting Rowan. She tells him after. He approves, with the dragon's silent agreement. Rowan does not use the crown in this chapter — the chapter's grief is felt, not paid.
  2. Two days of waiting at Cradle Shrine. Mira tells Rowan about her father; about the silver caches; about realising she has been living on the very money she said she was rejecting. She is going to go back. Rowan understands this is goodbye, slowly. Their conversation is the chapter's emotional center.
  3. Aldren's death reaches them by messenger from Hold-on-Iron. He died on the steps of his hall; the bond-recoil from a corrupted ritual he attempted alone (he tried to bind the dragon at distance, having heard from spies of the hatching — the corrupted oath had already begun to tear at him). His last words to Rowan, relayed: *do not let them do to you what I tried to do to it.*
  4. Varkesh arrives at the shrine with twelve men under a white flag, asking for parley as the Iron North asked once for the body of the dead. (Ch 8 plant harvests.) He calls Rowan *smith.*
  5. The negotiation. Slow. Three rounds:
     - First: Varkesh offers to call off the war if Vaelgard surrenders the dragon to him for destruction. Rowan refuses.
     - Second: Varkesh offers to call off the war if the dragon is bound to Vaelgard but only as defensive weapon, with northern oversight. Rowan refuses.
     - Third: Rowan proposes the dragon as sovereign. Treaty signed by Varkesh, Rowan, and the dragon — the dragon as a signatory. **Varkesh nearly walks out.** His second-in-command, *Captain Tovr*, has been at his shoulder the whole parley and openly objects: a treaty with a dragon as signatory is something the clans will *not* accept; Varkesh will return north to a confederacy that may not still recognise him. Varkesh has to silence Tovr in front of Rowan — a long, cold sentence in Norn that requires no translation. The dragon, watching, says nothing. The parley is one breath from ending. Varkesh asks the dragon a question — *what will you do, if we agree to this?* The dragon, in old-tongue mode, answers: it will leave Vaelgard. It will not be a weapon. It will not be a hostage. It will fly. Varkesh closes his eyes for the count of three and accepts. *(Varkesh's cost is concrete: he will return north having signed a treaty his clans did not authorise. Tovr will be the first to speak against him at the next council. Rowan understands this in the moment Varkesh closes his eyes.)*
  6. Varkesh, before leaving, speaks his great-grandmother's name aloud to Rowan. The chapter ends on Rowan's silence.
- **PLANTS:** None new.
- **HARVESTS:**
  - Aldren's tremor (Ch 4) → his death.
  - Varkesh executing his captain (Ch 6 reference) / white-flag scout (Ch 8) → Rowan's trust grounded.
  - The dragon's wrong-shadow (Ch 7) → Varkesh sees it during parley, understands what is at stake.
  - Mira's silver / refusal of wine (Ch 4) / coin in boot (Ch 3) → all close.
  - Brann's "you'll know what you're for when something asks for you" (Ch 1) → the dragon asks Rowan to break the bond at the very end of this chapter, off-page, in a sentence Rowan refuses to repeat to Mira.
- **EMOTIONAL ARC:** Pre-grief → arrival of grief (Aldren) → diplomacy → moral clarity.
- **STATUS:** unwritten

### Ch 12: "Oath-Breaking" (Rowan POV, ~2,900 words)
- **BEATS:**
  1. Cradle Shrine. Dawn. Mira present. Varkesh present (he has stayed for the ratification). The treaty in three hands — Rowan's, Varkesh's, the dragon's claw on the parchment in dragon-blood.
  2. Rowan and the dragon at the oath-stone alone. Mira and Varkesh withdraw — the dragon's request. The salt prepared on the day of the hatching is on the stone.
  3. The dragon, in the longest old-tongue mode passage in the book: tells Rowan it was not the last egg. Two more in unknown shrines. Tells him it has been preparing for this since the day it was born. Asks him to break the bond — to release it, to take the cost in one final payment.
  4. Rowan asks one question. (The book has earned this beat: the smith who at chapter 1 could only ask half-questions now asks a real one.) "If I do this, will I remember why I did it?" The dragon answers: "You will remember the choice. You will not remember its weight. You will remember that you loved, but not whom."
  5. Rowan agrees. He speaks the oath-breaking name. Old-tongue mode in prose. The cost:
     - His master's voice (he has been hearing Brann in his head for chapters, doesn't realise until it goes).
     - Mira's face (he can describe her hair, her stance, the bow — not her face. He will know her by sound and posture forever after).
     - His own name in his master's mouth (the way Brann said it in praise — gone).
     - Multiple smaller losses, scattershot.
  6. The dragon flies. The shadow corrects itself for the first time — body and shadow match.
  7. Rowan walks out of the shrine. Mira meets him. He does not recognise her face but he knows her voice, and he tries to smile, and it is a smaller smile than the one he had at chapter 1, and the book loves him for it.
  8. The third kneeling: Rowan kneels, briefly, by the oath-stone, alone, after the others leave. Not to anyone. He stands in a different way than he used to. The book ends on Mira and Rowan walking south, with Varkesh's twelve men at a respectful distance, and a cloud the shape of a wing crossing the eastern peaks, and the line: *He would not remember why he was sad. He would only know that he was. It was enough to walk south on.*
- **PLANTS:** None — final chapter.
- **HARVESTS:**
  - Salt at the hatching (Ch 7) → used in oath-breaking.
  - The dragon's name-avoidance (whole book) → never given. Earned silence.
  - Brann's "you'll know what you're for" (Ch 1) → fully earned.
  - Memory ladder → final descent.
  - All character arcs → resolved.
- **EMOTIONAL ARC:** Sacred preparation → choice → sacrifice → walking out smaller and free.
- **STATUS:** unwritten
