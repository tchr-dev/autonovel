## Geography

- Vaelgard is a mountain kingdom shaped like a broken hand. (world.md)
- Hold-on-Iron is the current capital, on the central plateau. (world.md)
- Caer Vael, the old capital, was burned 300 winters ago and lies in ruins north over the Pass of Caer Vael. (world.md)
- Cradle Shrine is half a day's walk from the Caer Vael ruins, along the old basalt road. (world.md)
- The Iron North camp is 2 days' walk down the northern slope from Cradle Shrine. (world.md)
- Brann's Forge is 4 days' walk south of Hold-on-Iron. (world.md)
- Marrow Field is 2 days off-road from Hold-on-Iron, on Rowan's route from Brann's Forge. (world.md)
- All shrines are built of black basalt and contain an oath-stone of the same material. (world.md)

### Location anchors (sensory canon)

- **Brann's Forge (village):** twelve houses around the smithy. Smithy is timber-frame, slate-roofed, with one wide hearth open to a beaten-earth common room where men drink *shrub* in the evenings. Smell: charcoal, slag, pine-smoke, beer. The bowyer's shop next door is boarded. (world.md)
- **Brann's Forge (smithy interior):** two anvils (one large for ore, one small for finishing), a quench-trough cut from a single basalt block (this matters — the village stands on basalt road), three pairs of tongs hung from a rafter, a coal-bin built into the north wall. No windows; light comes from the hearth and the open south door. (world.md)
- **Marrow Field:** wide low ground between two ridges, scrub-pine, snow patchy in late winter. A wayside cairn at the south edge with a small refugee mourning-banner (red cloth) tied to it. The shallow grave is twelve paces east of the cairn. (world.md)
- **Hold-on-Iron (city):** stone-and-timber, three concentric walls, the keep at the centre. Gate Rowan enters by is the South Gate — single arch, two guards, a wide flagstone forecourt where beggars sit. (world.md)
- **Hold-on-Iron (Aldren's hall):** rectangular, three wooden columns down each long side, no banners on the walls (a kingly austerity that reads as poverty until you understand it), a low dais at the far end with one chair on it (no throne — the throne was lost in the Burning and Aldren sits on the chair Maeri's brother sat on, and everyone knows it). One narrow window high in the south wall. Smell: woodsmoke, beeswax, the king's illness. (world.md, characters.md)
- **Pass of Caer Vael:** scree above the treeline, two days of climbing, wind that comes around the rocks at a wrong frequency — sounds like a chord rather than a noise. No animals at the highest point. The road is paved in *worn basalt* the same as the shrines — this is the old dragon-road. (world.md)
- **Caer Vael ruins:** glass-slag where the dragonfire melted stone. Three of the foundations still recognisable as *foundations*; the rest is scatter. No shelter; no firewood without descending. (world.md)
- **Cradle Shrine:** one room, ten paces by ten paces, basalt walls and a basalt roof slab. Door faces east. The oath-stone is centred — a slab waist-high, the size of a tabletop, with a bowl-depression worn smooth in its centre by three hundred winters of hands. The back wall has a niche, sealed with mortar at story start, where the egg lies in its basalt cradle. Outside: a wood-pile, a small clear spring, an axe. (world.md)
- **Iron North camp:** twenty thousand men in a tented array along the northern slope, smoke in three rings, picket lines of horses, a central command-tent with a black banner (the Iron North do not use red or white the way Vaelgard does — black for command, no other faction colours). (world.md)

## Magic System Rules — HARD

- Dragon magic is *true-naming* of natural things (mountains, rivers, storms, bloodlines, stones). (world.md)
- No human, alone, knows any true names. Only a dragon, or a human bonded to a dragon, can use them. (world.md)
- Bonding requires the Ashen Oath, sworn at a dragon-shrine, sealed by the dragon and the human laying their right hands together on the oath-stone. (world.md)
- The bond persists until one party dies. (world.md)
- If the dragon dies, the bonded human dies within minutes. (world.md)
- If the human dies, the dragon survives but cannot ever bond again. (world.md)
- Each use of a true name takes the bonded human's *most-loved* memory currently held. (world.md)
- The bonded human does not feel a memory go until they reach for it later. (world.md)
- The crown is a focus, not a name. It is forged of black basalt-iron at Cradle Shrine. (world.md)
- The crown has four points. Three are missing in the broken state Rowan finds; the fourth (tooth-shaped) channels *fire*. (world.md)
- The broken crown can focus only *cold, wind, stone* — not *fire* — for the entire book. (world.md, hard rule)
- Speaking a true name *unfocused* costs the human their voice for days, sometimes for life. (world.md)
- A dragon cannot be commanded by its true name. The bond is consent. The crown amplifies what the bond permits, not more. (world.md)
- Dragon eggs hatch only on contact with a *living bonded* human. The crown alone is not enough. (world.md)
- The corrupted oath that Aldren and his council know omits the consent clause. The full oath, known only to Caldus, includes it. (world.md, characters.md)
- Distance-binding (fragment-ritual) requires a chip of an oath-stone in the bearer's possession. Aldren has a thumb-sized chip from Cradle Shrine, smuggled out at the Burning. A fragment-binding refused by the dragon recoils through the fragment and kills the bearer. Aldren dies this way in Ch 11. The fragment is destroyed in the recoil. (world.md, hard rule 7)
- The first bond, three thousand winters ago, was sworn without oath, stone, or crown — only consent. The Ashen Oath formalised what was first informal. The basalt of the dragon's resting place became the first oath-stone. Folk knowledge held by Caldus only. (world.md, hard rule 8)

## Magic System Rules — SOFT

- Memory loss is precise but not surgical: a whole memory goes; facts remain. (world.md)
- Bonded humans dream in old-tongue. (world.md)
- True names can be partially known (kind first, specific later). (world.md)
- Dragons can refuse to use a name they know. (world.md)

## Character Facts — The dragon

- 300+ winters of conscious waiting in the egg. (characters.md)
- Hatches in Ch 7 at Cradle Shrine. (outline.md)
- At hatching: size of a large dog, scales like warm black glass, eyes the colour of slag. (characters.md)

## Political / Factional

- Vaelgard royal guard: 400 men, half at Hold-on-Iron, half on the southern road. (world.md)
- Aldren's council: three thanes — Reska, Olen, Brae. (world.md)
- The Iron North is a confederacy of clan-holds, not a kingdom. (world.md)
- Vaelgard and the Iron North have not formally been at war for ~30 winters; relations are by letter. (world.md, characters.md)

## Cultural

- The dead are buried with one coin in the mouth. (world.md)
- Pine smoke is not burned on the longest night. (world.md)
- Smiths salute by raising the hammer-hand. (world.md)
- Red banners are mourning. White is celebration. (world.md)
- Royal house wears red collar, grey wool: red-on-grey. (world.md)
- Vaelish is the southern language; Norn the northern; the old tongue belongs to no human. (world.md)
- Coalday is the late-winter festival when each parish lights all its hearths from one shared coal. The story opens the morning after Coalday. The reeve's arrival in Ch 1 is not coincidence — Aldren timed the search to fall on the day every smith is inventorying. (world.md)
- Brann's Forge: 12 houses + smithy, two charcoalers, three ore-runners, a boarded-up bowyer's shop, a baker who is the only literate person in the parish. No tavern; the smithy hearth is the village gathering point. (world.md)
- Iron North command tents fly black banners; they do not use red or white the way Vaelgard does. (canon.md, this iter)
- Mira's speech tic: *right* in place of *yes/good*, and finishing other people's trailed-off sentences. Both are noble-school habits she has not unlearned. The absence of *right* in Ch 11 is load-bearing. (characters.md, voice.md)
